Crop Disease Encyclopedia

Image Crop Disease Cause Pesticide Solution Organic Solution
Apple Apple scab Caused by the fungus Venturia inaequalis, thriving in wet conditions. Apply captan or sulfur-based fungicides. Use neem oil and improve air circulation.
Apple Black rot Caused by the fungus Botryosphaeria obtusa, spreading through infected leaves and fruits. Use fungicides containing thiophanate-methyl or captan. Prune infected branches and apply copper-based sprays.
Apple Cedar apple rust Caused by the fungus Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae, requiring juniper as an alternate host. Apply myclobutanil or mancozeb fungicides. Remove nearby juniper plants and use sulfur-based sprays.
Apple healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Regular monitoring and preventive fungicide application. Maintain proper spacing, prune dead branches, and ensure good air circulation.
Blueberry healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Ensure regular monitoring and apply preventive treatments if needed. Mulch with pine needles and ensure proper irrigation.
Cherry Powdery mildew Caused by Podosphaera clandestina, leading to white powdery spots on leaves. Use sulfur-based fungicides or myclobutanil. Apply neem oil or baking soda spray.
Cherry healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Preventive monitoring and fungicide application if necessary. Maintain good air circulation and avoid excessive humidity.
Corn Cercospora leaf spot Gray leaf spot Caused by the fungus Cercospora zeae-maydis, spreading in humid conditions. Use azoxystrobin or propiconazole-based fungicides. Rotate crops and ensure proper field drainage.
Corn Common rust Caused by the fungus Puccinia sorghi, appearing as reddish-brown pustules. Apply strobilurin or triazole fungicides. Grow resistant varieties and remove infected plants.
Corn Northern Leaf Blight Caused by Exserohilum turcicum, leading to elongated lesions on leaves. Apply fungicides containing mancozeb or azoxystrobin. Rotate crops and avoid planting maize in infected fields.
Corn healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Maintain proper pest control and apply preventive fungicides if needed. Ensure good spacing and irrigation practices.
Grape Black rot Caused by the fungus Guignardia bidwellii, forming brown spots on leaves and fruits. Use mancozeb or myclobutanil fungicides. Prune infected vines and ensure proper ventilation.
Grape Esca (Black Measles) Caused by a fungal complex affecting older grapevines, causing wood decay. No chemical treatment available; remove infected vines. Apply sodium bicarbonate and improve soil health.
Grape Leaf blight (Isariopsis Leaf Spot) Caused by Pseudocercospora vitis, forming small brown lesions on leaves. Apply copper-based fungicides. Ensure good air circulation and remove infected leaves.
Grape healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Monitor regularly and apply preventive treatments as needed. Maintain good pruning and fertilization practices.
Orange Haunglongbing (Citrus greening) Caused by Candidatus Liberibacter spp., spread by Asian citrus psyllids. Apply systemic insecticides like imidacloprid. Use neem oil and introduce natural predators of psyllids.
Tomato Bacterial spot Caused by Xanthomonas spp., leading to dark, water-soaked leaf spots. Use copper-based fungicides and bactericides. Apply compost tea and avoid overhead watering.
Tomato Early blight Caused by Alternaria solani, forming brown concentric rings on leaves. Use chlorothalonil or copper-based fungicides. Mulch soil to reduce spore splash and remove infected leaves.
Tomato Late blight Caused by Phytophthora infestans, leading to rapid leaf decay. Use mancozeb or metalaxyl-based fungicides. Remove infected plants and use resistant varieties.
Tomato healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Regularly monitor for pests and apply fungicides as needed. Ensure crop rotation and proper ventilation.
Peach Bacterial spot Caused by Xanthomonas arboricola, leading to small, dark lesions on leaves and fruits. Use copper-based bactericides and streptomycin sprays. Prune infected branches and avoid overhead watering.
Peach healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Regular monitoring and preventive treatments if needed. Ensure proper air circulation and balanced fertilization.
Pepper Bell Bacterial spot Caused by Xanthomonas campestris, leading to black water-soaked lesions. Apply copper-based fungicides and bactericides. Use disease-free seeds and rotate crops.
Pepper Bell healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Preventive monitoring and fungicide application if necessary. Ensure proper irrigation and soil health.
Potato Early blight Caused by Alternaria solani, forming concentric ring spots on leaves. Use chlorothalonil or azoxystrobin fungicides. Apply compost tea and rotate crops.
Potato Late blight Caused by Phytophthora infestans, leading to rapid leaf decay and rot. Use mancozeb or metalaxyl-based fungicides. Remove infected plants and ensure good ventilation.
Potato healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Monitor regularly and apply preventive treatments if needed. Ensure proper drainage and fertilization.
Raspberry healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Preventive monitoring and fungicide application if necessary. Maintain good pruning and pest control.
Soyabean healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Monitor for early signs of disease and pests. Use crop rotation and natural pest deterrents.
Squash Powdery mildew Caused by Podosphaera xanthii, forming white powdery spots on leaves. Use sulfur-based fungicides or potassium bicarbonate. Apply neem oil and improve airflow around plants.
Strawberry Leaf scorch Caused by Diplocarpon earliana, leading to reddish-brown leaf spots. Use fungicides like myclobutanil or captan. Ensure good soil drainage and remove infected leaves.
Strawberry healthy No disease detected, plant is in good condition. Regular monitoring and preventive treatments if needed. Maintain good watering practices and remove weeds.
Tomato Leaf Mold Caused by the fungus Passalora fulva, leading to yellowing and mold growth on leaves. Apply chlorothalonil or copper-based fungicides. Increase air circulation and avoid overhead watering.
Tomato Septoria leaf spot Caused by Septoria lycopersici, forming small, dark leaf spots with light centers. Use mancozeb or chlorothalonil fungicides. Remove infected leaves and practice crop rotation.
Tomato Spider mites Two-spotted spider mite Caused by Tetranychus urticae, leading to yellow stippling and webbing on leaves. Apply miticides such as abamectin or bifenthrin. Use neem oil and encourage natural predators like ladybugs.
Tomato Target Spot Caused by Corynespora cassiicola, leading to dark lesions on leaves and fruits. Use azoxystrobin or copper-based fungicides. Ensure proper spacing and remove infected plant parts.
Tomato Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus Caused by a virus transmitted by whiteflies, leading to curled and yellowed leaves. Use imidacloprid or pyriproxyfen for whitefly control. Use reflective mulches and introduce predatory insects.
Tomato Tomato mosaic virus Caused by Tomato mosaic virus (ToMV), leading to mottled, distorted leaves. No direct chemical control; remove infected plants. Disinfect tools and avoid handling plants when wet.
